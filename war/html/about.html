Help: Navigating the Website

When opening the website, you should be greeted with a list of units (one through six) covering various Android topics. These units are the primary blocks of the website and where you will be spending most of your time. These units are broken into several sections, typically three. To access these sections you must click the appropriate button found at the bottom of the page (the blue circle thing.) Additionally, there are about pages and a resources page you can explore! Most pages are directly accessible through the navigation bar at the top of every page. Within this navigation bar you can expand units to choose a specific topic or expand the about for the appropriate pages there. Finally, if you would like to quickly find something or are having trouble locating it, try using the search box at the top of the page.

If you are still having trouble with something, don't hesitate to contact me!

Help: Units

This course is broken into six sections: Introduction to Android, Creating your First App, More about Widgets, Organizing it all Together, Final Projects and Publish.

The first unit is intended to help you get started with Android development. It demonstrates how to setup an Android Development Environment with Eclipse and discusses terminology for Android development.

In the second unit second unit a few Android widgets are described and you are expected to make your first Android application. An optional project and test accompany this unit.

For the third unit, a plethora of Android widgets are explored to give the student more breadth in their Android abilities. After this, the necessary layouts and Activities for developing Android applications are explained. Optional quizzes are supplied for the third section of the unit.

In the fourth unit, the student's Android abilities are solidified as this is the final learning unit available. The 'glue' of Android applications are discussed such as the Android Manifest, Intents, SharedPreferences, Content Provider, ActionBar and tabs.

Once the fifth unit is reached, the student is expected to have already amassed a variety of Android application skills and is expected to build their first solo project as well as their first group project.

Finally, the sixth unit is intended to help publish those great Android applications that were just created. Where and how to publish Android applications are covered as well as how to make money on those apps!
